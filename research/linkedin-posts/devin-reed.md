## Post 2
**Date:** May 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_if-youre-a-b2b-marketer-drowning-in-requests-activity-7455315156588318720-ZJ3R?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFJkeLEBrvXP2UgDDYzykAJzS1BbJ8VM1rI
**Content:** If you're a B2B marketer drowning in requests and wondering what to prioritize — read this.

I've led and advised dozens of B2B content teams and the hardest part is never the content itself. 

It's knowing where to start when everything feels urgent.

My career changed once I figured out how to handle the overwhelm. 

Here's what's helped me:

𝟭. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗖𝗘𝗢 𝗸𝗲𝗲𝗽𝘀 𝗮𝘀𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗳𝗼𝗿 "𝗼𝗻𝗲 𝗺𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴" 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗴𝘆 𝗶𝘀 𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝗮 𝘄𝗶𝘀𝗵𝗹𝗶𝘀𝘁...
→ Start with CEO goals, not your content calendar

The first question isn't "what should we create?" It's "what are our top three company priorities — and how does content impact them?" Everything else flows from there. This makes your job easier and your CEO happier.

𝟮. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂'𝗿𝗲 𝗯𝗲𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗮𝘀𝗸𝗲𝗱 𝘁𝗼 𝗰𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗲 𝗠𝗢𝗥𝗘 𝗰𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘄𝗼𝗻𝗱𝗲𝗿𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗵𝗼𝘄 𝘁𝗳 𝗮𝗺 𝗜 𝗴𝗼𝗻𝗻𝗮 𝗱𝗼 𝘁𝗵𝗮𝘁...
→ Repurpose like hell and build a content waterfall

This is the fastest way to win. One great piece of content — webinar, podcast, report — can fuel LinkedIn posts, blogs, and email all at once. Starting from scratch should be your last resort.


𝟯. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂'𝗿𝗲 𝗱𝗼𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘁𝗼𝗼 𝗱𝗮𝗺𝗻 𝗺𝘂𝗰𝗵 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗱𝗼𝗻’𝘁 𝗸𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝘄𝗵𝗮𝘁’𝘀 𝘄𝗼𝗿𝘁𝗵 𝗸𝗲𝗲𝗽𝗶𝗻𝗴...
→ Measure growth, not just outputs

Pull the data on what's working. If something isn't driving pipeline, audience growth, or brand — kill it or replace it with something better. You could probably stop half your content today and no one would notice. That's not a failure, that's being lean. 



I spent years drowning in the same requests. Turns out the answer was never more content — it was knowing exactly what to say no to.

## Post 4
**Date:** April 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_when-i-became-head-of-content-i-learned-activity-7444424058240364544-7fjK?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFJkeLEBrvXP2UgDDYzykAJzS1BbJ8VM1rI
**Content:** When I became Head of Content, I learned the hardest part wasn’t writing—it was working with the executive team.

After leading content at two $1B+ SaaS companies and advising dozens of founders, here’s what I wish I knew sooner:

The real challenge? Bridging the gap between vision and execution. It wasn’t about copy, campaigns, or conversion rates. 

It was about trust.

Here’s what I’ve learned:


1️⃣ Turn “We need more content” into “Here’s what actually works”

Instead of reacting to endless content requests, reframe the conversation.

-From “We need more leads”
To: Let’s double down on what’s converting best.

-From: “We should be on every channel”
To: Here’s where our audience actually spends time.

-From “We need to go viral”
To: Let’s create something worth talking about—consistently.

Your job isn’t just to produce—it’s to set and protect the strategy.


2️⃣ Recognize the “Marketing vs. Sales” Tension

Sales wants pipeline now. 
But Marketing is playing the long game.

Your job? Balance both without caving to short-term thinking.

Push back when needed. But always have quick wins on the calendar. 


3️⃣ Speak Executive (Not Marketer)

I used to walk into meetings with engagement metrics. Nobody cared.

Now I start with:
-How content influences revenue
-What’s next (and why)
-Invite questions and input 

Save the click-through rates for the marketing team.


4️⃣ Pick Your Battles (And Win the Right Ones)

Not every content request deserves a fight.

But some hills are worth fighting on—like positioning, messaging, and brand narrative.


Don’t let “random acts of content” kill your strategy.

Learn how to operate like an executive. 

It’ll help get you more budget and creative freedom you need to win.
