## Post 2
**Date:** April 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_here-are-the-10-once-valuable-advantages-activity-7445822779251240961-Ui5S?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFJkeLEBrvXP2UgDDYzykAJzS1BbJ8VM1rI
**Content:** Here are the 10 once-valuable advantages that are now rapidly declining in economic value:

1. Memorizing information - Information is no longer a scarce resource. The market is repricing domain knowledge, and it's declining rapidly. Collecting more of it is no longer the sustainable advantage it once was. 

2. Grinding and overworking - Overworking leads to poor decision-making, cognitive overload, lower creativity, emotional reactivity, stress, and burnout. The economic consequences are becoming so obvious. 

3. Following best practices - By the time something becomes a "best practice" it's already obsolete. The people who think for themselves win. 

4. Execution without clarity - Executing flawlessly on the wrong things costs you years. And then the sunk cost fallacy sets in, and it's hard to turn around. Action without clarity is like driving fast down the highway with no map or destination. 

5. Collecting credentials to signal competence - Credentials represent knowledge and competence that is no longer scarce. The value the credential represents is no longer valuable. 

6. Incremental optimization - Optimizing a broken system just gets you to the wrong destination faster and more efficiently. 

7. Networking for volume - A thousand shallow connections are worth less than five deep ones. Depth is the new currency. 

8. Consuming content to get ahead - More information without subconscious training produces knowledge without real change. You understand more but operate the same. 

9. Single domain specialization - AI masters any single domain faster than any human. The edge is now thinking across domains and at the intersections. 

10. Delegating your thinking to “experts” - Outsourcing your judgement and decisions to coaches and thought leaders is a dependency, not a strategy. 

__

All of these were legitimate, effective strategies 30 years ago. So much so that many people confuse them as facts. But they aren't facts, they are strategies built on assumptions of how the World works and those assumptions are no longer true. 

Want to create sustained advantages and command economic premiums in the future? The first step is to throw out the old playbook and start operating based on how the world works TODAY, not how it worked in 1997.

**Content:** One of the biggest realizations I ever had: Discerning the difference between a fact and a belief. 

It sounds obvious. It isn’t. And it quietly runs your entire life until you know the difference. 

The confusion between them is one of the biggest invisible drivers of stress, limitation, dissatisfaction and stuckness, regardless of how successful you look on paper. 

–

A FACT is something everyone would agree to.

The grass is green. That door is open. The tide went out.

These are neutral, observable, and not up for debate.

A BELIEF, on the other hand, is something that not everyone would agree to. 

But most people treat their beliefs as facts. And that's where things go sideways.

"You need to suffer to be successful."

"The workweek is Monday through Friday.”

"Money is hard to get and there's not enough of it."

"Success is measured by money and achievements."

"Alcohol is how you celebrate and relax."

"I'll be happy when I reach ___."

Every single one of these is a belief. Not a fact. But most people live their entire lives as if these are laws of nature.

__

Here's why this matters:

Your beliefs are the filters for how you experience and interpret everything. Every decision you make. Every emotion you feel. Every opportunity you see or miss. 

Two people can experience the exact same event and walk away with completely different emotions, interpretations, and actions.

Same event. Different beliefs. Different experience.
__

And here's what nobody tells you:

You didn't choose most of your beliefs. They were handed to you by family, culture, school, media, and social conditioning. And then reinforced through repetition until they stopped feeling like beliefs and started feeling like facts.

That's programming.

The moment you learn to distinguish a fact from a belief, everything shifts. Not because your circumstances change. Because you realize that the invisible rules running your life were never rules at all.

They were always optional. 

At any moment, you can rewrite the rules.

## Post 5
**Date:** February 2026 
**URL:**  https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_life-frequency-growth-activity-7425923361970745344-qdz0?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFJkeLEBrvXP2UgDDYzykAJzS1BbJ8VM1rI
**Content:** In 2021, I’d built a $5MM rapidly growing business and was experiencing mid-grade anxiety multiple times a week that would throw off my entire day. 

I remember telling myself:

“I’m just an anxious person.” 
“My job is stressful, and this just happens to me sometimes” 
“Entrepreneurship is hard. This is normal.” 

For years, I justified and accepted these symptoms, not only as “normal” but also as a part of who I am. 

What I didn’t realize at the time is that my anxiety wasn’t random. It was cause by my unexamined, threat-based beliefs about how the World works. 

This is what most commonly accepted advice teaches us to do. 
Manage the symptoms. Cope. Push through with willpower. Stop, breathe, “allow” the feeling and keep going. 

But none of this actually stops it from coming back next time. 

__

Now, I haven’t experienced any level of anxiety in the past 18 months. Zero. 

How? 

By training the most important life skills that nobody ever taught me to learn. 

1. Metacognition - Awareness of thought. Being the observer of your own thoughts. 

Example: You become consciously aware of a thought like “I’m falling behind,” instead of automatically believing it. 

2. Cognitive Awareness - Understanding the impact of thought. The recognition that thoughts drive emotions, actions, and results. 

This is where real leverage happens. Responsibility without shame. Empowerment for our life. And ownership of our emotions. 

Example: You realize the thought “I’m falling behind” creates anxiety. And that anxiety creates urgency. And urgency leads to rushed decisions, people pleasing, and burnout. 

3. Belief Reappraisal - The intentional evaluation and updating of beliefs based on whether they actually serve you. 

This is where real change happens. 

Example: You trace “I’m falling behind” back to the belief that life is a race and you’re not enough unless you’re ahead. 

You consciously choose a belief that’s more accurate and more empowering: “I’m right where I’m supposed to be. I progress at the pace that’s right for me.” 

And then feel calm, confidence, and spaciousness, which leads to clarity, aligned decisions, and sustained momentum. 

This isn’t surface-level "positive thinking". It’s continuous operating system upgrades through intentional structured training. 

__

Sounds simple? It is. And it’s incredibly effective. 

These are skills that any human can learn. Just like learning to read or learning how to drive. It just requires intention and training. And the impact compounds over time. 

Developing these skills have had such a positive impact on my quality of life that I feel compelled to share and invite anyone who’s curious to explore them for themselves
